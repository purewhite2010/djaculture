About Me

I'm an Aboriginal from the Ugarapul tribe, Queensland, Australia, who teaches, educates, and entertains in Aboriginal cultural form. I follow traditional methods, skills and customs and use natural materials for all practical components.

The workshops show the diversity of all activities of how the ecological lore works.

Ecological lore is the sustainability of the Ugarapul Aboriginal lifestyle learning how we live on, from and with the land.

Elon University, North Carolina

Teaching eco sustainability• 2004 to Present

Eco Sustainability in Ugarapul Aboriginal terms involves knowing the many different aspects of basic knowledge in,

  • Tribe
  • People / area
  • Language
  • Traditional pathways
  • Geographical features
  • Fauna / flora
  • Tools / weapons
  • Trade routes
  • Neighbours - tribe / language
  • Hunting grounds
  • Ceremonial places
  • Cultural significance ( lore )
  • Camp sites
Ecological lore governs WHY, WHEN and WHERE.

Ecological Program

Limit of 30 people: $10,000 (Additional costs for Douglas James and staff in accomodation and meals)

The Ecological program operates October through to April over 3 days.

Ecological program is a theory and practical based experience, visiting Aboriginal Cultural sights, playing traditional games, throwing boomerangs and a refreshing swim, after returning with more activities on the first day, with the remainder of the two days at the venue fulfilling the program that will build to a dancing ceremonial Corroborree finishing in the evening on the last day.

Participation in:

  • Traditional games
  • Ecological lore
  • Fauna and flora
  • Early settlement - European disturbance
  • DVD presentation of kangaroo for food, string and hide
  • Didgeridoo
  • Fire, string, basket, musical instrument and necklace making
  • Ochre-crushing, facials, painting
  • Aboriginal cultural display
  • Story telling
  • Song and dance
  • Boomerang throwing
  • Nut cracking / bush tucker (food, depending on season)
  • Permission of acknowledgement - passing through ones country
  • Language - of neighbouring tribes
Transport is required for the first day, can be provided at additional cost
